Ken Burns: The Art of Storytelling

Assignment: Analyze the principles of a master filmmaker.

Instructions

Answer the questions below using information and quotes from the video "Ken Burns on the Art of Storytelling." Your answers should be thoughtful and well-supported.

1. The Role of Place

According to Burns, how has his choice to live and work in Walpole, New Hampshire, influenced his approach to storytelling? Explain the concept of "splendid isolation" and its importance.

2. Narrative Structure

Burns states that a good story embraces contradiction. What does he mean by this, and how does he apply this principle to his work on American history?

3. Filmmaker as Historian

Burns sees his role as honoring the "whole wild unformed thing" of human events. In your own words, what does this mean for a filmmaker's responsibility when telling a historical story?

4. The Power of Discovery

Burns prefers to dive into subjects he doesn't know enough about. Explain why he considers this "process of discovery" to be so crucial to his filmmaking and to the audience's experience.

5. Personal Connection

Burns shares a story about a man and his brother who used his baseball film to reconnect. What is the significance of this anecdote to Burns' view on success and the ultimate purpose of his work?

Additional Task: Your Turn to Tell a Story

Reflect on your own storytelling experiences. What is a story you have told, or would like to tell, that embodies one of Ken Burns' principles? Explain your choice and how the principle would be applied.
