What is Pre-Production? ‚ú®

Pre-production is an essential planning stage for any project, from commercials to feature films. It's the period where you lay the groundwork for a successful and organized shoot. While it can be overwhelming, staying organized makes it simple! The goal is to define how efficient your production will be, and the more work you put in here, the smoother your on-set experience will be. Remember to **KISS** - **K**eep **I**t **S**hort and **S**imple!

Why This Matters üí°

The skills you learn in pre-production directly connect to the course's learning outcomes:

  • 1
    Create clear and organized plans: Locking the script, finalizing the budget, and creating a shooting schedule are the foundation of a clear plan.
  • 2
    Produce awesome audio and video sequences: Storyboarding and shot listing help you visualize and plan your sequences before you ever start filming.
  • 3
    Effortlessly manage and save files: Pre-production is where you plan your file management system and folder structure, so you can stay organized from the start.
  • 4
    Work together with others: Hiring crew, casting actors, and holding tech scouts are all collaborative activities that ensure everyone is on the same page.
  • 5
    Skillfully record audio and video: Arranging for equipment rentals and performing a tech scout ensures you have the right gear for the job.
  • 6
    Be comfortable using editing software: By planning your project in detail, you make the editing process much smoother and more efficient.
